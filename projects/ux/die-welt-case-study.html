Product launch content: Longform Case Study

tldr; Longform customer case study to pair with Chartbeat’s 2017 subscriber analytics product launch.

buzzwords: case study, research, writing, product launch.

When Chartbeat announced subscriber analytics in August 2017, bringing it to market involved a lot of moving pieces (as all go-to-market plans do), the piece I was most proud to own was a piece of product launch content explicitly tied to the value of measuring subscribers—a longform case study with the German national daily, DIE WELT.

This project was fun for me—not only was it a great opportunity to think more deeply about how to pair different kinds of collateral with product launches but it was genuinely a pleasure to write.

Collaborating with Chartbeat’s Director of Product Marketing, I first received all the primary sources I had to work with: a video of DIE WELT presenting at a conference, a factsheet going over their company’s history and KPIs, and our own collection of user interviews we conducted.

Exerpt

From there I pitched a couple of different thesis ideas to our Product Marketing Director, she and I agreed on the one we felt was the strongest narrative and aligned most with the positioning of the subscriber analytics capability in the product, and sketched out areas of support.

And then it was left to me to write—always my favorite part. Fast-forward several rounds of edits, getting the copy approved by our partners at DIE WELT, and funneling it through our Marketing Designer, and we were ready to go-to-market.
