Charley Site

tldr; Personal webiste design and development for entrepreneur Charley Todd.

tools: Flexbox, HTML&CSS, JQuery

Our first assigment in New York Code + Design Academy's Front-End 101 bootcamp was a one page website and my friend/entrepreneur/writer/social butterfly Charley Todd needed a webiste for his personal brand. So that was easy.

Even better Charley had already created his own style guide complete with brand colors, fonts, language guidelines, and illustration and layout styles. Armed with that, working up an on-brand design was realatively straightforward.

Design credit to Focus Lab for the style guide.

One of the things I've been working on is integrating something new into projects I take on and in this case I was excited to use Flexbox. Even when I started really getting the hang of CSS I still had a hard time with floating—and if I'm being honest I still don't 100% get it.

When I brought it up to one of my developer friends, he simply said, "why are you even still using float? Just use Flexbox". After which I promptly googled Flexbox and rejoiced beacause it makes so much sense.

CSS-Tricks has a really great overview of the syntax that I still constantly reference, and there's a cute game called Flexbox Froggy that I'd highly recommend.

Design, design, design.

I think I'll always like design but one of the best things about this bootcamp has been getting to really dive into JavaScript and the core technical aspects of front-end development. Of course developing for the web will always have a big crossover with design (which is great for people who also enjoy design) but I'm most excited that my instincts to follow coding have been right on. I remember from the design class that I took that I would get tired of trying to solve design problems after a while. Code problems just make me happy.

"Final" version live! I'm also learning that nothing is really ever the final version.
